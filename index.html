About

Learning in public, building with intent, growing 1% better every day!

Open source changed the way I learn. What began as curiosity about web development grew into contributing to real-world projects at Sugar Labs, understanding large codebases, and writing tests for legacy systems.

I also submitted a GSoC proposal for Music Blocks v3 maintenance. Although it didn’t receive a slot, the experience taught me how to read projects deeply, communicate with maintainers, and think long-term about contributions.

I’m part of my college’s Open Source Club, where I work closely with the head repo maintainer and support students in making their first meaningful contributions.

Beyond code, I express myself through writing poems and pieces on self help, exploring creativity as a complement to my technical pursuits.
